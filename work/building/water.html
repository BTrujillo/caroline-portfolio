WATER AS PUBLIC SPACE

studio: design I, fall 204

critic: frederick tang

site: north williamsburg, new york

Waterfront space is a paradox in light of surging seas and climate change. As the water level rises, the New York waterfront continues to be developed for luxury residential buildings. The expensive pricing of the East Ferry system reflects the expensive nature of areas directly located near the waterfront. The North Williamsburg terminal specifically has new high rise development and park space that toes the line between public and private. This new terminal proposal aims to create a new public park on the water, freed from the looming shadow of intense real estate capital while developing an awareness for the rising water as ferry riders make the transition from land to water. The transactional ticketing space is separated from the park in order to allow for casual visitors. A system of tubing feeds water from the ticketing space through the public seating and the network of troughs to simulate the rising water level to enhance the experience of a waterfront space in an urban context.
