LUDIC GREEN LAB

studio: design I, fall 2014

critic: kadambari baxi

site: east harlem, new york

The Mosaic Preparatory Academy 375 is one of many co-located schools within the East Harlem neighborhood sharing space with a charter school due to lack of real estate for public schools. This case of co-location has resulted in a distribution of spaces between three groups, the public school, a special education program, and a Success Academy charter school. The lack of interaction between students due to the spatial division is an example of the tension resulting between public and charter school institutions within the larger debate of the New York City educational system. The project proposes the design of an edible schoolyard incorporating a kit of parts strategy to create an environment combining urban gardening and education in a series of flexibly programmed spaces in order to maximize the potential of the space and foster more open interactions. Due to the flexible system, the space aims to inspire play as a fluid interaction within the educational program both for children attending the schools and the larger community.
